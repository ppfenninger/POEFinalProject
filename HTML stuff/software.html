Explanation of challenges

We didn’t have enough money for a good quadrature encoder, and other solutions like potentiometers (are Gross and) don’t allow for full rotation. A stepper motor wasn’t a viable solution because while the stepper only knows its angular position correctly when not moved by an outside force.

Our solution: we made our own cheap rotary encoder out of four [$__] IR reflectance sensors and a printed encoder sheet that we made in MS paint. [ ENCODER IMAGE HERE captioned “Graphic design is my passion, in comic sans] Each 30° section of the encoder differs by one bit from both of the ones next to it (except the 11 and 12 positions), and the reflectance sensors each read a different bit from outside (0001) to inside (1000).
